Sensory Garden Access Project

Co-designing inclusive experiences for blind, deaf and deaf-blind visitors in botanical gardens.

Introduction

Botanical gardens are temples of biodiversity—yet too often they remain inaccessible to visitors with sensory impairments. Our multidisciplinary team partnered with Glasgow Botanic Gardens and the broader community to re-imagine inclusive design through low-cost, high-impact prototypes.

Our Research

Field Interviews

15 blind / deaf participants shared real-world barriers and desired multisensory cues.

Site Walk-throughs

We shadowed visitors, logged navigation pain-points and audio-gaps.

Benchmarking

We analysed 8 leading gardens worldwide (Kew, Brooklyn, Madrid, etc.).

Co-creation Workshops

Three rapid prototyping sprints with guides, horticulturists and disability advocates.

Project Steps

  1. Discover – empathise with users
  2. Define – synthesise pain-points
  3. Ideate – prototype concepts
  4. Test – iterate with users
  5. Deliver – final toolkit

Conclusion

By combining tactile, auditory and visual channels, our toolkit transforms a garden visit from exclusion into exploration. The prototypes are low-cost, scalable and ready for local adaptation—proving that inclusive design can be beautiful and sustainable.
