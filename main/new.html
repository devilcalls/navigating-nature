Introduction

The Friends of Glasgow Botanic Gardens offer free seasonal tours, delivered by volunteer guides who share knowledge about plants, wildlife, history, and artworks. Our project aims to make these tours accessible for deaf and blind visitors by understanding their needs and proposing adapted materials for an inclusive experience.

Our Research

Field Interviews

Interviewed 15 blind/deaf participants; found deaf users need BSL, blind users rely on textures and audio.

Site Walk-throughs

Shadowed visitors, noted navigation barriers (e.g., lack of tactile cues) and audio gaps.

Benchmarking

Studied 8 gardens (e.g., Kew’s tactile maps, Brooklyn’s audio guides) for best practices.

Co-creation Workshops

Held 3 sprints with guides and disability advocates to brainstorm solutions.

Project Steps

  • Discover – empathize with users
  • Define – synthesize pain-points
  • Ideate – sketch prototype concepts
  • Refine – iterate designs with feedback
  • Deliver – propose final toolkit

Prototypes & Sketches

Textured guidebook with tactile shapes and braille for blind visitors.

QR-code boards linking to BSL videos and audio descriptions for deaf/blind users.

Website with accessible video/audio content for all users.

Conclusion

Our proposed toolkit, including a tactile guidebook, QR-code boards, and an accessible website, aims to transform Glasgow Botanic Gardens tours into inclusive experiences for deaf and blind visitors. These low-cost, scalable solutions are designed for easy adoption by volunteer guides, promoting sustainable accessibility.
