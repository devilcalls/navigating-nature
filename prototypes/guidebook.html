The Textured Guidebook

Portable Sensory-Rich Companion for Glasgow Botanic Gardens

📋 Project Prototype Notice

Important: This Textured Guidebook is a conceptual prototype developed as part of an academic project for Glasgow Botanic Gardens. This is not a currently available product or service. The design, features, and specifications described represent proposed solutions for improving garden accessibility through innovative multi-sensory materials.

Project Overview: The Textured Guidebook is a revolutionary portable, multi-sensory companion designed for self-guided tours of Glasgow Botanic Gardens. This innovative publication combines tactile illustrations, integrated Braille, and scratch-and-scent technology to create an inclusive experience for all visitors.

🌟 Innovative Approach

Unlike traditional garden maps or guides, the Textured Guidebook represents a completely new concept for Glasgow Botanic Gardens. This groundbreaking accessibility tool doesn't replace existing materials—it creates an entirely new category of visitor experience that has never been offered before at the gardens.

Design Purpose

More than just a map, the Textured Guidebook serves as a beautifully designed, tactile companion that allows visitors to experience the shapes, textures, and even scents of the garden's collection. It bridges the gap between visual observation and multi-sensory exploration, ensuring that every visitor can form a deep, personal connection with Glasgow's botanical heritage.

This innovative guide transforms the traditional garden visit into an immersive journey where visitors can feel the intricate patterns of a fern frond, understand the architectural beauty of the Kibble Palace through raised illustrations, and experience the distinctive scents of different garden zones—all while learning about the garden's rich 200-year history.

Key Features

✋

Tactile Illustrations

Raised-line drawings of key plants and structures allow visitors to feel the shape of leaves, the texture of bark, the layout of garden zones, and architectural details of iconic buildings like the Kibble Palace.

⠃

Integrated Braille

Complete descriptions of garden zones, plant histories, and featured specimens are provided in Grade 2 Braille alongside standard printed text, ensuring equal access to information.

👃

Scratch and Scent Panels

Specially treated panels release authentic scents when scratched—pine in the conifer section, roses in the rose garden, herbs in the medicinal plant area—adding an olfactory dimension to the experience.

💰

Sustainable Revenue Model

Free for visitors with visual or hearing impairments, while available for purchase as a premium souvenir for all visitors, creating a self-sustaining accessibility program.

Textured Guidebook Cover

Example Page: GIANT OF THE ANDES

This is a beautifully designed two-page spread from "A Sensory Tour of Glasgow Botanic Gardens", a textured guidebook created to be accessible for visitors with visual impairments.

The Kibble Palace: History and Architecture

Brings the iconic glasshouse to life through touch. A visitor can read its history in large print or Braille and then feel the intricate, raised patterns of its famous wrought-iron and glass structure.

The Rose Collection: Blooms and History

Focuses on the sights and smells of the rose garden. It describes the seasonal blooms and allows a visitor to feel the soft, velvety texture of a rose's petals, then experience its classic fragrance with a "scratch and scent" panel.

The Medicinal Garden: Traditional Uses

Explores the world of medicinal plants like lavender. A visitor can learn about its traditional uses and then interact with the page by touching the raised lavender stalks and releasing their calming aroma from the "scratch and scent" area.

Native Woodlands: Ecology and Conservation

Connects visitors to the local woodland ecosystem. It tells the conservation story of native plants and features a rich tactile landscape where one can feel the rough texture of bark, the soft surface of moss, and the delicate shape of a bluebell flower.

🎁

User Experience Journey

Following the Orchid House Route

Scenario: A visitor with visual impairment follows the recommended route to the orchid house using the Textured Guidebook.

Navigation: Using the tactile map, they locate their current position and trace the path to the orchid house, feeling raised pathways and landmark indicators.

Arrival: Upon reaching the orchid house, they open the relevant page to read detailed descriptions in Braille about the collection's history and significance.

Tactile Exploration: They run their fingers over raised illustrations showing the unique shapes and growth patterns of specific orchid varieties, understanding forms they cannot see or touch directly.

Scent Experience: Scratching the designated panel releases the delicate fragrance of orchid blooms, adding an olfactory dimension to their understanding.

Deep Connection: Through this multi-sensory approach, they develop a comprehensive understanding of the orchids that goes beyond what traditional visual-only materials could provide.

  • Educational Enhancement: Provides deeper botanical and historical context than traditional signage allows
  • Revenue Generation: Self-sustaining model supports ongoing accessibility improvements
  • Marketing Value: Unique offering that distinguishes Glasgow Botanic Gardens as an accessibility leader
  • Keepsake Appeal: Beautiful design makes it a cherished memento of the garden visit
  • Scalable Technology: Successful model can be adapted for other botanical gardens and museums

Vision for Glasgow Botanic Gardens

The Textured Guidebook represents more than an accessibility tool—it embodies a new philosophy of inclusive design that enriches every visitor's experience.
