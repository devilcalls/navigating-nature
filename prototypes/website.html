The Accessibility Website

Central Digital Hub for Glasgow Botanic Gardens Accessibility Initiative

📋 Project Prototype Notice

Important: This Accessibility Website is a conceptual prototype developed as part of an academic project for Glasgow Botanic Gardens. This is not a currently implemented or available platform. The website design, features, and WCAG compliance specifications described represent proposed solutions for improving digital accessibility and visitor experience at the gardens.

Project Overview: The Accessibility Website serves as the central digital hub that underpins the entire Glasgow Botanic Gardens accessibility initiative. This WCAG-compliant platform provides comprehensive support for visitors before, during, and after their garden experience through rich multimedia content and assistive technology integration.

🌟 Digital Innovation for Glasgow Botanic Gardens

This represents a groundbreaking digital accessibility platform specifically designed for Glasgow Botanic Gardens. The website doesn't replace existing digital resources—it creates an entirely new category of inclusive digital experience that has never been offered by the gardens before.

📱 QR Code Integration with Information Boards

The Accessibility Website serves as the digital destination for all QR codes featured on our Accessible Information Boards. When visitors scan QR codes at physical exhibits, they're instantly connected to rich multimedia content hosted on this platform—creating a seamless bridge between physical and digital accessibility features.

Design Purpose

Built to the highest accessibility standards (WCAG AA compliance), this comprehensive digital platform ensures that every visitor to Glasgow Botanic Gardens can access information in their preferred format. The website serves as both a planning tool and a real-time companion, transforming smartphones into powerful accessibility devices that provide personalized experiences for visitors with diverse needs.

The platform integrates seamlessly with our physical Accessible Information Boards, creating a unified ecosystem where digital and physical accessibility features work together to provide unprecedented access to the gardens' botanical knowledge and historical heritage.

Key Features

🗺️

Pre-Visit Planning

Comprehensive accessible maps detailing routes, sensory exhibits, accessible facilities, and assistance beacon locations. Interactive planning tools help visitors customize their experience based on their specific accessibility needs.

🎥

Sign Language Video Guides

Dedicated video tour section featuring professional interpreters using both British Sign Language (BSL) for local visitors and American Sign Language (ASL) for international guests, with full captions on all content.

🎧

Audio Content Hub

Central destination for QR code scans from Information Boards. Each code links to professionally narrated audio descriptions, allowing visitors to use their own smartphones and headphones as personal audio guides.

📝

Complete Transcripts

Every video and audio element includes full, easily accessible text transcripts, ensuring that content is available in multiple formats to meet diverse accessibility needs and preferences.

WCAG Compliance & Accessibility Standards

Meeting Highest Accessibility Standards

The platform is designed to exceed WCAG 2.1 AA standards, ensuring compatibility with all major assistive technologies and providing an excellent experience for users with diverse abilities.

🔍

Screen Reader Compatible

Full compatibility with JAWS, NVDA, and VoiceOver screen readers through semantic HTML and ARIA labels.

⌨️

Keyboard Navigation

Complete keyboard accessibility with logical tab order and visible focus indicators throughout the site.

🎨

Visual Accessibility

High contrast ratios, scalable text up to 200%, and color-blind friendly design choices.

📱

Mobile Optimized

Responsive design ensures excellent accessibility across all devices and screen sizes.

Implementation Benefits

  • Universal Access: WCAG compliance ensures the platform works with all assistive technologies
  • Seamless Integration: QR codes create smooth connection between physical Information Boards and digital content
  • Personal Device Compatibility: Visitors use their own smartphones and headphones as accessibility tools
  • Cost-Effective Solution: Leverages existing visitor devices rather than requiring expensive audio guide equipment
  • Multilingual Potential: Platform architecture supports expansion to multiple languages including Gaelic
  • Real-Time Updates: Digital content can be updated instantly without replacing physical signage
  • Analytics & Improvement: Usage data helps identify popular content and areas for enhancement

Content Integration with Information Boards

QR Code Experience Example

Scenario: A visitor approaches the historic Kibble Palace area and encounters one of our new Accessible Information Boards.

Physical Board: Provides essential information in large print and Grade 2 Braille about the Palm House's Victorian architecture and plant collection.

QR Code Scan: Instantly connects to a dedicated webpage featuring:

  • Professional audio narration describing the intricate ironwork and towering palms
  • BSL video tour explaining the historical significance of the 1873 structure
  • Complete transcript of all audio and video content
  • High-resolution images with detailed alternative text descriptions
  • Links to related exhibits and historical context throughout the gardens

Revolutionary Impact for Glasgow Botanic Gardens

🌟 First-of-Its-Kind Digital Accessibility Initiative

This Accessibility Website represents a groundbreaking advancement for Glasgow Botanic Gardens—the first comprehensive digital accessibility platform in the garden's 200+ year history. Unlike existing basic website information, this platform creates an entirely new category of visitor experience that has never been available before.

Current Gap: Glasgow Botanic Gardens currently lacks any dedicated accessibility website or QR-code integrated content system. Visitors with disabilities must rely solely on basic printed materials and limited signage.

Our Innovation: This platform fills a critical accessibility void by providing the first-ever comprehensive digital support system specifically designed for visitors with diverse abilities, transforming how people experience and engage with the gardens.

Future Development Potential

  • Seasonal Content Updates: Platform can showcase changing plant displays, seasonal highlights, and garden events
  • Community Contributions: Framework allows for user-generated content and visitor story sharing
  • Educational Partnerships: Integration with school programs and university research initiatives
  • Augmented Reality Ready: Architecture supports future AR features for enhanced plant identification
  • Conservation Messaging: Platform ideal for sharing garden's conservation work and environmental initiatives
  • Event Integration: Accessibility features for special exhibitions, concerts, and educational programs

Long-Term Vision

The Accessibility Website establishes Glasgow Botanic Gardens as a leader in inclusive cultural experiences. By seamlessly connecting physical accessibility features with cutting-edge digital content, we create a model that other botanical gardens and cultural institutions can follow.

This platform transforms visitors' smartphones into powerful accessibility tools while preserving the natural beauty and peaceful atmosphere of the gardens. The result is a more inclusive, educational, and engaging experience that honors both the garden's rich heritage and its commitment to serving all members of the Glasgow community.

🌟 The Complete Accessible Experience

Together with our Accessible Information Boards, this website creates Glasgow Botanic Gardens' first fully integrated accessibility ecosystem—where every visitor, regardless of ability, can discover, learn, and connect with 200+ years of botanical wonder and Scottish garden heritage.
