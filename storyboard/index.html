Experience Nature for Everyone

Discover Glasgow Botanic Gardens through our specially designed accessible tours and resources for visitors with visual and hearing impairments.

Accessibility First: This website and all our services are designed with accessibility in mind. All content is screen reader compatible and includes alternative text for images.

Our Accessibility Services

📚

Textured Guidebook

Download our free accessible guidebook with tactile maps, plant descriptions, and garden history. Available in multiple formats including Braille.

📡

Accessibility Beacon

Personal safety device that instantly connects you with trained staff. Press the button and help will be guided directly to your location.

👥

Guided Tours

Small group tours (max 5 people) led by specially trained guides. Available once or twice monthly with personalized attention for each visitor.

🤟

Sign Language Support

Our guides receive optional ASL and BSL training to better serve deaf and hard-of-hearing visitors throughout their garden experience.

📱

Interactive Lecterns

Weather-resistant information stations with text, Braille, and QR codes linking to sign-language videos with captions and audio descriptions.

🚶

Independent Exploration

Visit anytime with full accessibility support. All services available for both guided tours and self-directed exploration.

How Your Visit Works

1

Pre-Visit

Download the free Textured Guidebook or collect it at the gardens to familiarize yourself with the layout and exhibits.

2

Arrival

Receive your personal accessibility beacon at the main entrance for instant connection to our trained staff.

3

Explore

Join a guided tour or explore independently. Use QR codes at lecterns for multimedia content in your preferred format.

4

Support

Access immediate help anytime by pressing your beacon button. Our trained staff will be guided directly to you.

5

Departure

Return your beacon at the main gate. Take home your guidebook as a lasting memory of your visit.
